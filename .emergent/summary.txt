The Sanhaja Travel Agencies application is a multi-agency accounting system for Algeria, offering a bilingual UI (Arabic/French).
**Initial Core Requirements:** Role-based authentication, agency-isolated database, CRUD interfaces for entities, key screens (dashboard, invoicing, payments, reports), multi-agency views for managers, notifications, and user/system management.

**New Requirements & Enhancements Implemented during this Trajectory:**
1.  **Daily Operations Management**: Enhanced with advanced filtering (agency, service type, client name, date range, amount, status) and an approval workflow allowing modifications based on status and user roles.
2.  **Professional PDF Printing**: Generated branded PDFs (invoices, receipts, daily reports) with agency logo, address, and electronic staff signature, with added support for Arabic fonts and accurate partial payment details.
3.  **Receipt Preview Modal**: A modal to preview receipts with agency details, service type, payment, amounts, and electronic signature, updated to display correct payment values.
4.  **Agency-specific Settings**: Allow input and modification of detailed agency information (contact numbers, addresses, registration details), with role-based access.
5.  **Service Cash Flow Module**: Independent module for tracking service sales, cash delivery to accountants, confirmation of cash receipt, and reconciliation reports.
6.  **Redesigned Payment System**: Payments are now directly linked to daily operations, replacing the previous invoice-based system. It includes an intuitive UI for partial payments, automatic remaining amount calculation, and clear payment status indicators.
7.  **Service Installments Module**: A new module to create flexible installment plans for service sales, supporting custom due dates, partial payments, plan modification/cancellation (without penalties for overdue), and status reports.
8.  **Role-Based Access Control Refinement**: Implemented stricter permissions, restricting Agency Staff from viewing invoices and bookings, focusing their access on daily operations and associated payments. Super Admins gained control over agency management.
</product_requirements>
